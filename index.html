The Cornell Crime Logs

By Beatrice Jin

Like many universities (examples), Cornell University keeps a public log of incidents reported to the Cornell University Police Department. The log is published almost daily, but few people know about it. As of today, Nicholas Bogel-Borroughs remains the only person to be tweeting from the hashtag #CornellCrimeLog. This visualization is an attempt to make this information more accessible to students and faculty.

The following graph shows the number of reported incidents that occur each day. Note that weekends are in grey. There seems to be a spike in reports right before Halloween...

When sorted by location, it seems like Barton Hall (16 incidents), Mary Donlon Hall (10 incidents), and the Cornell Store (9 incidents). Is this more evidence for Donlon's reputation as a party dorm and homework cheaters?

Note: The Cornell Crime Logs are erased past two months from the current date. For this reason, there is only data from about two months ago as of today.
